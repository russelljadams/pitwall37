# Lateral and Longitudinal Load Transfer
**Source:** Suspension Secrets
**URL:** https://suspensionsecrets.co.uk/lateral-and-longitudinal-load-transfer/

## Lateral Load Transfer

Lateral load transfer occurs during cornering and is the shift of mass across the wheels due to the centrifugal force and the lateral acceleration.

### Key Equation

**dW = (W x Ay x h) / t**

Where:
- dW = Load transfer amount
- W = Total vehicle mass
- Ay = Lateral acceleration in g-force
- h = Center of gravity height
- t = Track width

### Practical Example

A vehicle cornering at 0.9g with a center of gravity height of 0.6m and track width of 1.6m experiences 33.75% lateral load transfer — meaning 166.88kg shifts to the outer wheels during a 1000kg vehicle's turn.

## Longitudinal Load Transfer

Longitudinal load transfer occurs during acceleration or braking, transferring mass between front and rear axles.

### Basic Equation

**dWx = (W x Ax x h) / L**

Where:
- dWx = Front-to-rear load transfer
- L = Wheelbase
- Ax = Longitudinal acceleration in g-force

### Example Calculation

Using a 1000kg vehicle with 0.6m center of gravity height and 2.7m wheelbase under 1G acceleration: 222.22kg transfers from front to rear wheels.

## Complex Longitudinal Transfer

When the center of gravity is offset laterally, individual wheel load transfers must be calculated separately using offset ratios (e) and specific coefficients for each wheel position.

## Practical Application

Understanding load transfer helps engineers tune suspension geometry (anti-dive, anti-squat, anti-lift) to manage ride height changes during dynamic maneuvers.
